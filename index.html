The Open Kitchen

Ocaq — the hearth

Everything begins
at the hearth.

In Azerbaijani, ocaq means the hearth — the fire at the centre of the home, and, by the same word, the family gathered around it. We took the name because it is the truest description of what we do.

Here in Baku — the Land of Fire, where flames have risen from the earth for three thousand years — we cook the old way. Wood is lit hours before you arrive. Every dish that matters is finished over its embers.

01 — Odun · The Wood

It starts
in the dark.

Hours before the first guest, a single match meets oak, grapevine and apricot wood. The kitchen fills with the scent of the orchard before it fills with anything else.

02 — Köz · The Embers

Then we
wait.

Flame is impatient; embers are wisdom. We let the fire fall to glowing köz — even, steady, fierce. Only then does anything touch the heat.

03 — Tüstü · Smoke & Char

Smoke
does the rest.

Lamb, sturgeon, vegetables from the morning market — each is read by hand and given exactly the fire it asks for. A char here, a whisper of smoke there. Nothing is hurried.

04 — Süfrə · The Table

And then,
it's yours.

Carried straight from the ember to your süfrə — the Azerbaijani table that means generosity before it means furniture. This is the whole of OCAQ: fire, and the people gathered around it.
